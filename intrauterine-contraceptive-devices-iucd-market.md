<p><ul><li>Non-hormonal</li><li>Hormonal</li><li>Inert</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Intrauterine Contraceptive Devices (IUCDs) are available in three main types: Non-hormonal, Hormonal, and Inert. Non-hormonal IUCDs work by preventing fertilization through the release of copper ions. Hormonal IUCDs release progestogen to prevent fertilization and thicken cervical mucus. Inert IUCDs do not release any hormones or chemicals and prevent fertilization by creating a physical barrier. Each type of IUCD offers women different options for long-term contraception, catering to their individual needs and preferences.</p></p>

<p><p>Intrauterine Contraceptive Devices (IUCD) are widely used as a form of long-acting reversible contraception. T-shaped IUCDs are one of the most common types, designed to prevent pregnancy by releasing copper ions that are toxic to sperm. V-shaped IUCDs, on the other hand, are shaped like a V and work by preventing the fertilized egg from implanting in the uterus. Both types of IUCDs are effective and convenient methods of birth control for women looking for a long-term solution.</p></p>

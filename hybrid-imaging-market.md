<p><p>Hybrid Imaging refers to the combination of different medical imaging modalities to acquire comprehensive and detailed information about a patient's condition. There are three main types in the market: PET or CT, SPECT or CT, and PET or MRI. PET/CT combines positron emission tomography (PET) and computed tomography (CT) scans, providing both anatomical and functional information. SPECT/CT combines single-photon emission computed tomography (SPECT) and CT scans, enhancing the accuracy of image interpretation. PET/MRI combines PET and magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) scans, offering precise anatomical and functional imaging capabilities. These hybrid imaging technologies are valuable in diagnosing diseases, guiding treatment decisions, and monitoring therapy effectiveness.</p></p>

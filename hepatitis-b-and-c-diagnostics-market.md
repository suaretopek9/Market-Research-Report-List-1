<p><p>Hepatitis B and C are viral infections that primarily affect the liver. Hepatitis B is caused by the hepatitis B virus (HBV) and can be transmitted through blood and other body fluids, while hepatitis C is caused by the hepatitis C virus (HCV) and is mainly transmitted through contact with infected blood. Both infections can lead to chronic liver disease and potentially fatal complications such as cirrhosis and liver cancer.</p><p>Hepatitis B and C diagnostics involve various tests and procedures to detect and confirm the presence of HBV and HCV in individuals. These diagnostics include serological tests that detect viral proteins and antibodies, nucleic acid tests that detect viral genetic material, and imaging techniques to assess liver damage. Early and accurate diagnosis plays a crucial role in the management and treatment of hepatitis B and C, as it allows for timely intervention and monitoring of the disease.</p><p>The Hepatitis B and C Diagnostics Market is projected to grow at a CAGR of 6.00% during the forecast period. There is a growing demand for point-of-care testing (POCT) devices and rapid diagnostic tests, as they offer quick and convenient results. Furthermore, there is an increasing focus on developing novel biomarkers and non-invasive tests for the early detection and monitoring of hepatitis B and C.</p><p>Overall, the Hepatitis B and C Diagnostics Market is expected to experience substantial growth in the coming years, driven by the increasing prevalence of viral hepatitis and advancements in diagnostic technologies.

<p><p>Solar salt is commonly produced through two main market types: sea water and underground brine. Sea water solar salt is obtained by evaporating seawater in large ponds, resulting in pure salt crystals. On the other hand, underground brine solar salt is derived from underground salt deposits, where brine is extracted and then evaporated to obtain salt. Both market types offer a sustainable and cost-effective source of salt for various industries, such as chemical production, water treatment, and food processing.</p></p>

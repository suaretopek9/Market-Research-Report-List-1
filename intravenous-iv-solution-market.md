<p><p>Intravenous (IV) solutions are categorized into Total Parenteral Solutions (TPS) and Peripheral Parenteral Solutions (PPS) based on their composition and intended use. TPS are designed to provide all the essential nutrients and electrolytes needed for patients who cannot consume food orally, while PPS are used for short-term hydration and medication delivery in patients who are able to eat but require additional fluids. Both types of IV solutions play a crucial role in maintaining hydration and providing necessary nutrients in healthcare settings.</p></p>

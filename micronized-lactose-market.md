<p><p>Micronized lactose is a finely powdered form of lactose which is commonly used as a filler or diluent in pharmaceuticals. The two main types of micronized lactose are lactose monohydrate and lactose anhydrous. Lactose monohydrate contains one molecule of water per molecule of lactose, while lactose anhydrous is completely water-free. Both types are widely used in the pharmaceutical industry for their flow properties and compatibility with active ingredients. They are also used in the food industry as a sweetener and bulking agent.</p></p>

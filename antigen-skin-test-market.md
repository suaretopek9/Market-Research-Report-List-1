<p><p>The antigen skin test, also known as a skin prick test or a puncture test, is a diagnostic tool used to determine allergies in individuals. It involves applying a small amount of allergen extract on the skin and then pricking or puncturing the skin with a lancet or needle. If the person is allergic to that specific allergen, a small raised bump or wheal will appear on the skin within minutes.</p><p>The antigen skin test is commonly used to diagnose allergies to substances such as pollen, mold, animal dander, dust mites, and certain foods.

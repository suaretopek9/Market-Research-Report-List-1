<p><p>Electronically Controlled Limited-Slip Differential (ECLSD) systems are used in vehicles to improve traction and power distribution between wheels. There are three main types of ECLSD systems: Electronic Hydraulic, Electromagnetic, and Electric Mechanical. Electronic Hydraulic systems use hydraulic pressure to control the differential locking, while Electromagnetic systems rely on electromagnets to adjust the torque distribution. Electric Mechanical systems utilize electric motors to regulate the differential locking. Each type offers unique advantages and is suited for different driving conditions and vehicle applications.</p></p>

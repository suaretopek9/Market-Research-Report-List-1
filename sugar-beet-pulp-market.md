<p><p>The sugar beet pulp market includes dried pulp, wet pulp, and pressed pulp. Dried pulp is obtained by drying the extracted pulp, resulting in a longer shelf life and easier storage. Wet pulp is the fresh byproduct of sugar beet processing, commonly used as animal feed due to its high moisture content. Pressed pulp is created by pressing the wet pulp to remove excess liquid, making it more compact and cost-effective for transportation. Each type serves a unique purpose in various industries within the sugar beet market.</p></p>

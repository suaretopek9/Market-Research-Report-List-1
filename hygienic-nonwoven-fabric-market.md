<p><p>Hygienic nonwoven fabric market types include spunlaced, heat sealing, dry papermaking, meltblown, and other market segments. Spunlaced fabrics are created by entangling fibers with high-pressure water jets, while heat sealing involves bonding fibers using heat. Dry papermaking utilizes air to form the fabric, while meltblown fabric is made by blowing melted polymer fibers onto a conveyor. Other market segments may involve unique production methods or materials, catering to specific hygiene needs and preferences. Each type offers distinct benefits and applications in the hygiene industry.</p></p>

<p><strong>Market Segmentation</strong></p>
<p><strong>The Automotive Emission Analyzer Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Non-dispersive Infrared (NDIR) Analyzers</li><li>Flame Ionization Detector (FID)</li><li>Chemiluminescence Analyzer (CLA)</li><li>Constant Volume Sampler (CVS)</li><li>Other Type</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Non-dispersive Infrared (NDIR) Analyzers use infrared light to measure emissions of gases like CO, CO2, and hydrocarbons. Flame Ionization Detector (FID) measures total hydrocarbons by burning the sample in a hydrogen flame. Chemiluminescence Analyzer (CLA) detects NOx by measuring the light emitted during a chemical reaction. Constant Volume Sampler (CVS) collects a fixed volume of exhaust gas for analysis. Other types include Electrochemical Sensor Analyzers, Paramagnetic Analyzers, and Fourier Transform Infrared (FTIR) Analyzers, each with unique methods for measuring automotive emissions.</p></p>

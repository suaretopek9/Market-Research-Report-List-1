<p><p>The microbial cell analysis instrument market is comprised of several types of instruments. Flow cytometers are used to analyze individual cells in a fluid stream. NGS systems enable high-throughput DNA sequencing. PCR instruments amplify and detect specific DNA sequences. Spectrophotometers measure the absorption and emission of light, commonly used for quantifying nucleic acids and proteins. Microscopes visualize microbial cells for analysis. Cell counters are used to determine cell concentration and viability. HCS systems combine microscopy and automation for high-content analysis. Cell microarrays are used to study cell behavior on a large scale. Other instruments include incubators, shakers, and centrifuges.</p></p>

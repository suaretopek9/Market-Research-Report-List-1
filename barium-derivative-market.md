<p><p>Barium derivatives are divided into four main market types: Barium Carbonate, Barium Chloride, Barium Nitrate, and Barium Sulfate. Barium Carbonate is commonly used in the manufacturing of ceramic glazes and glass. Barium Chloride is widely used in the production of pigments, dyes, and pharmaceuticals. Barium Nitrate is primarily utilized in fireworks, explosives, and the manufacturing of other barium compounds. Barium Sulfate is commonly used in the oil and gas industry as a weighting agent in drilling fluids and in the medical field as a contrast agent in X-ray imaging.</p></p>

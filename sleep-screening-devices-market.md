<p><p>Sleep screening devices are classified into two types: wearable devices and portable devices. Wearable devices are designed to be worn on the body during sleep and provide continuous monitoring of sleep patterns and vital signs. They are typically worn on the wrist or head and are convenient for long-term monitoring. On the other hand, portable devices are compact and user-friendly, allowing individuals to conduct sleep screenings at home. They are easy to carry and can measure various sleep parameters, such as breathing rate and sleep duration. Both types of devices contribute to early detection and management of sleep-related disorders.</p></p>

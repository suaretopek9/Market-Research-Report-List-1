<p><p>Hypoglycemic drugs refer to medications used to treat low blood sugar levels in people with diabetes. The market for these drugs can be categorized into various types. Insulin is a commonly used hypoglycemic drug that helps regulate blood sugar levels. DPP-4 inhibitors work by increasing the production of insulin and reducing the production of glucose in the liver. GLP-1 agonists stimulate insulin secretion while lowering glucagon levels. SGLT-2 inhibitors prevent the reabsorption of glucose by the kidneys, promoting its excretion. Other types of hypoglycemic drugs include sulfonylureas, meglitinides, and alpha-glucosidase inhibitors, which work through different mechanisms to control blood sugar levels.</p></p>

<p><p>Tractor attachments and implements can be categorized into two main types: powered and unpowered. Powered attachments are those that require an external power source, such as a tractor's engine, to operate. This can include implements like mowers, sprayers, and hay balers. On the other hand, unpowered attachments do not require an external power source and are driven solely by the movement of the tractor. This category includes implements like plows, rakes, and seeders. Both powered and unpowered attachments play essential roles in enhancing the efficiency and functionality of tractors in various agricultural tasks.</p></p>
